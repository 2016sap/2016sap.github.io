Pushing boundaries


Step In @ July 28, 2016

step in 2016

"Step In" is the second annual open house at the Innovation Center Silicon Valley. Come learn about how the ICSV uses Machine Learning to push boundaries with customers every day.

Explore disruptive technologies and trends in the Silicon Valley, engage in an interactive design thinking workshop about Pokémon GO, and learn how we scale Machine Learning through customer engagements.

There will be a HanaHaus pop-up café with delicious Blue Bottle Coffee, as well as a variety of prizes, giveaways, and snacks. Hope to see you there!

our people

We explore unconventional viable ideas, engage strategic customers, drive customer-led innovation, and scale innovation by embracing the entrepreneurial spirit within and beyond the SAP ecosystem right from the heart of the Silicon Valley.

We embrace diversity and individuality — in background, talent, and identity.

Our team members hail from all over the world, including countries such as India, Germany, Japan, and Hungary. We're data scientists, Machine Learning experts, UX designers, and much more.

The core of the Innovation Center is its people, and together we make the ICSV, ICSV.

industries we've worked in

Big Data

Big Data is an opportunity to change how you work, play, and live by tracking new signals within the digital hoise. From major league sports and personalized shopping, to cancer treatments trailored to your DNA, SAP customers are achieving what was once impossible.

Machine Learning

Machine learning is a critical component of Big Data. With machine learning, we can empower our customers to preduct the future in real time so that they act faster and gain competitive advantage.

Urban Development

Every urban development team want to have better engagement with its citizens, to provide best real time services to them and want to have a connected community by bringing the citizens and urgan autohrities together. The data for enabling such platform emerges from government offices, factories, schools, traffic, and residences to name a few.
